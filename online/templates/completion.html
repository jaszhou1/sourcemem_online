Thank you for participating in a project on decision-making in source memory. This research aimed to investigate the nature of how people remember the context in which items are encountered. In particular, we are interested in whether memory for context is "all or nothing", or if memory precision varies continuously.

You were asked to study a list of words and memorise the words and the location of those words around a circle. Each word represented an item, and each location represented the contextual information for that item.

Although the experiment can be quite difficult and repetitive at times, we hope that in undertaking the experiment, you were able to reflect on how these different parts of memory might interact when we retrieve information from memory.
