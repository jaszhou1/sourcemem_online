Introduction

We would like to invite you to participate in a project on visual short-term memory and decision making. This research aims to investigate how information is stored in visual short-term memory when attention is divided, and how this affects decision-making processes. We examine these issues under different decision-making conditions, including two-choice responses and responses in a continuous domain using eye or hand movements.

What I will be asked to do?

Should you agree to participate, you will be asked to complete a single experimental session of approximately 45 minutes in length. In this session you will be asked to complete simple computer-based experiments requiring a perceptual judgment about stimuli that will be briefly presented on a computer screen.

Throughout the task you will be asked to respond as quickly and accurately as possible. Each session consists of multiple smaller blocks, between which you can take a break.

What are the risks?

Whilst performing the task is repetitive, it is not difficult, and no significant discomfort or harm is expected to result from participation in these activities. It is possible that you will experience mild discomfort due to the repetitiveness of the task, and if you feel that your mood has been negatively affected you can contact a psychology counselling service such as LifeLine (13 11 14) or the University of Melbourne Psychology Clinic (Tel: 03 9035 5180; email: clinic@psych.unimelb.edu.au).

What are the benefits?

This study will produce new insights into the capacity limitations of visual short-term memory, and will likely result in conference presentations and articles in scholarly journals. Funding for this project has been provided by the Australian Research Council.

If you agree to participate, this will satisfy part of your course credit for the Research Experience Program (REP).

How will my confidentiality be protected?

If you choose to participate in this study, your responses in the experiment will be recorded, stored and processed by the researchers in password protected files. These responses may form part of later published journal articles or be presented at academic conferences. Your confidentiality will be protected subject to legal limitations.

The nature of the project means that the information provided will not be sensitive (consisting of reaction times and accuracy data concerning simple perceptual judgments), and cannot be used to infer sensitive data. Your browser version, IP address, and screen resolution will be automatically determined and stored with your data to determine compatibility with the experiment.

What if I want to withdraw from the Research?

Participation in this research is completely voluntary. You are free to withdraw at any time and to withdraw any unprocessed data previously supplied. This would have no effect on your relationship with any member of the School of Psychological Sciences. It would not affect your grades, assessment or any treatment that you would otherwise be eligible for.
